Key Terms and Acronyms


In the field of Value Based Healthcare (VBHC), understanding key acronyms is essential for effective communication and application of concepts. This section provides a concise reference to the various acronyms and terms you’ll encounter throughout the course.

Key terms and acronyms

Familiarising yourself with these acronyms will help you navigate the terminology and enhance your learning experience. From PROMs to PREMs, each abbreviation is crucial for grasping the fundamental principles of VBHC.

ACRONYM / TERM EXPANDED FORM DEFINITION
VBHC Value Based Healthcare A transformative approach prioritising patient outcomes (quality) over service volume (quantity).
PROMs Patient Reported Outcome Measures PROMs are defined as standardised, validated questionnaires completed by patients to measure their perception of their health status, functional wellbeing and quality of life.
DROMs Device Reported Outcome Measures DROMs involves using data directly collected from medical devices to evaluate a patient's health status, treatment outcomes, or quality of life. DROMs use objective data from devices like wearables, implants, or monitoring systems.
PREMs Patient Reported Experience Measures PREMs are short questionnaires that collect direct feedback from the patients throughout their health care journey. It reflects their experiences with healthcare services, providing insights into satisfaction and the quality of care.
CPDs Continuing Professional Development CPD refers to the ongoing education and training that healthcare professionals engage in throughout their careers to maintain and enhance their skills, knowledge, and competencies.
ASLM The Australasian Society of Lifestyle Medicine ASLM is a health promotion charity working towards improved prevention, management, and treatment of chronic, complex, and lifestyle related conditions. ‘Lifestyle related’ includes environmental, societal, behavioural and other factors.
PRMs Patient Reported Measures Functions as the compass for navigating this journey, meticulously capturing the invaluable insights of those we are dedicated to serving – the patients.
AI Artificial Intelligence AI refers to the simulation of human intelligence processes by computer systems, including learning, reasoning, problem solving, and decision making.
KOOS The Knee Osteoarthritis Outcome Score The Knee Osteoarthritis Outcome Score (KOOS) is a specific tool used to assess the impact of knee osteoarthritis (OA) on a patient’s quality of life.
QOL Quality of Life Quality of Life (QoL) refers to a broad and multidimensional concept that encompasses an individual’s overall wellbeing and life satisfaction, particularly in the context of their health status.
PCO Patient Centred Outcomes Patient Centred Outcomes refer to the results and measures that reflect the experiences, preferences, and values of patients concerning their healthcare and treatment.
ICHOM International Consortium for Health Outcomes Measurement The ICHOM is a global organisation dedicated to improving the quality of healthcare by defining and measuring health outcomes that matter most to patients.
DiME The Digital Medicine Society The Digital Medicine Society (DiME) is a non-profit organisation focused on advancing the field of digital medicine.
EROM Employing Routine Outcome Monitoring EROM in healthcare refers to the systematic and regular assessment of patient outcomes to track and improve the effectiveness of treatments and interventions.
PIP Practice Incentives Program A practical approach involves creating an alternative reimbursement pathway, distinct from existing frameworks.
EHRs Electronic Health Records EHRs are digital versions of patients' paper charts and medical histories. EHRs are used by healthcare providers to manage and store patient information electronically, enhancing the efficiency and quality of care.
HMOs Health Maintenance Organisations HMOs are a type of managed care organisation designed to provide comprehensive and coordinated healthcare services to their members.
KPI Key Performance Indicator KPI is a measurable value that helps organisations track and evaluate their progress toward achieving specific goals and objectives.
ACOs Accountable Care Organisations Groups of doctors, hospitals, payers and other health care providers who voluntarily come together to provide coordinated high quality care to their patients.
PCMHs Patient Centred Medical Homes Emphasising primary care as the central component for managing patient health, focusing on accessibility, long term relationships and comprehensive care.
IPUs Integrated Practice Units Teams that provide full cycle care for a patient’s condition, enhancing coordination and outcomes.
UMC University Medical Centers In a healthcare context, UMC often refers to University Medical Centers. These are large, academic hospitals or health systems associated with universities or medical schools.
AI/ML Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) are transformative technologies with increasing applications in healthcare, including medical diagnostics, treatment planning, and administrative tasks.
NSW New South Wales NSW stands for New South Wales, a state located on the east coast of Australia.
AHHA Australian Healthcare and Hospitals Association It is a leading national peak body representing public and not-for-profit hospitals and health services across Australia.
HIEs Health Information Exchanges Initiatives to facilitate the sharing of health information across different systems and organisations.
Payers Payers In the context of Value Based Healthcare in Australia, payers refer to organisations such as Medicare, private health insurers, and workers' compensation insurance that finance or reimburse healthcare services, with a focus on aligning payments with the quality of care and patient outcomes.
PROM Scales and Subscales PROM Scales and Subscales In the context of VBHC and PROMs, scale refers to the overall measurement tool or instrument used to assess a specific health outcome or quality of life aspect, while subscale denotes a specific component or dimension within that overall scale that measures a particular aspect of the broader construct. For example, a PROM might include a scale for overall health status, with subscales assessing physical functioning, mental health, and social wellbeing, allowing for a more detailed analysis of patient outcomes and experiences.
