What And Why

Definition:

Digital transformation in healthcare entails the adoption of technological tools and processes to enhance patient care, streamline operational procedures, and facilitate informed decision making.

Relevance:

With the healthcare sector facing an array of challenges ranging from rising costs to increasing patient expectations, digital transformation emerges as a pivotal solution. It not only addresses current challenges but also positions healthcare providers to adapt to future developments.

Historical Evolution

The journey of digital transformation in healthcare has been evolutionary, marked by milestones like the advent of Electronic Medical Records (EMRs) and telemedicine. These advancements have progressively shaped a more efficient and patient centric healthcare landscape.

1970s

Adoption of Electronic Health Records (EHRs)

In the late 20th century, healthcare providers began transitioning from paper records to electronic health records, setting a foundation for digital healthcare.

1980s

Development of health informatics

The field of health informatics began to grow, focusing on the efficient and effective acquisition, management, and use of information in healthcare.

Introduction of Picture Archiving and Communication Systems (PACS)

PACS emerged to store and access radiological images digitally, enhancing diagnostic imaging services.

2000s

Health Information Exchanges (HIEs)

Initiatives to facilitate the sharing of health information across different systems and organisations began to take shape.

Mobile Health (mHealth) applications

The proliferation of smartphones led to the development of mobile health applications that allow for remote monitoring and management of health conditions.

2010s

Wearable health technologies

Wearables like fitness trackers and smartwatches have become prevalent for monitoring physical activity, sleep, and other health-related metrics.

Cloud computing

Adoption of cloud computing has facilitated data storage, sharing, and analytics in healthcare.

Big data and analytics

The ability to collect and analyse large datasets, including Patient Reported Outcome Measures (PROMs) and Patient Reported Experience Measures (PREMs), has enabled better understanding and prediction of health outcomes.

3D printing

Used for creating medical devices, prosthetics, and even tissue engineering.

2015

Artificial intelligence and machine learning

AI and ML technologies have found applications in diagnostic imaging, predictive analytics, and personalised medicine.

Digital therapeutics

Utilising digital tools to treat medical conditions and support overall healthcare management.

Blockchain for health data security

Exploration of blockchain technology for ensuring the security and integrity of health data.

2019

Telemedicine expansion

Saw substantial growth with better internet connectivity, especially accentuated during the COVID-19 pandemic.
