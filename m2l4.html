Implementation: best practices and strategies

The systematic and routine use of PROMs and PREMs is increasingly recognised as essential for evaluating the quality and performance of health services and systems. While substantial advancements have been made in the development and evaluation of these measures, their adoption and implementation in healthcare settings could become challenging.

A Comprehensive Framework For Integrating PRMs In A Healthcare/Practice Setting

In this section, we present a framework for implementing Patient Reported Measures (PRMs) in healthcare. This concise guide leads healthcare professionals through the essential steps of PRM integration, from initial planning to execution. It’s designed to embed PRMs effectively into patient care, enhancing outcomes and innovating healthcare delivery. This framework serves as both a practical roadmap and an insightful resource for transforming patient engagement and care.

Step 1

Training and education

To ensure understanding and effective use of PRMs, implement training programs for clinicians/healthcare staff and provide knowledge based educational initiatives for patients.

Step 2

Pre implementation assessment

Assess your healthcare setting’s needs and capabilities, and evaluate existing workflows and systems for PRMs integration.

Step 3

Strategic selection of PRM tools

This entails choosing a mix that balances generic measures with condition specific and body part specific ones, alongside PREMs to capture patient care experiences and cared for perspectives.

Step 4

Stakeholder engagement

Engage key stakeholders, including clinicians, patients, and administrative staff, to ensure successful PRM implementation and buy in. Understand their perspectives and foster collaboration.

Step 5

Integration with healthcare IT systems

Address the technical aspects of integrating PRMs with existing healthcare IT systems, most importantly Electronic Health Records (EHRs). Focus on interoperability, user friendliness, and minimal disruption to clinical workflows.

Step 6

Data privacy and security

Ensure compliance with data protection laws and prioritise patient data privacy and security. Implement measures to safeguard the confidentiality and integrity of PRMs data.

Step 7

Pilot testing

Conduct pilot tests to evaluate the PRMs process, gather preliminary data, and identify areas for improvement before wider implementation.

Step 8

Full scale implementation

Strategically deploy PRMs across the healthcare setting. Establish monitoring and evaluation mechanisms to assess their impact and effectiveness via a smart VBHC PRMs automation IT system.

Step 9

Continuous improvement and adaptation

Regularly analyse PRMs data, gather feedback, and adapt to changes in healthcare trends and patient needs. Ensure PRMs remain effective and relevant.

How Do PROMs And PREMs Work Together?

PROMs and PREMs are complementary tools in healthcare that collectively provide a holistic view of patient care. PROMs evaluate the effectiveness of treatments based on patient reported health outcomes. PREMs, on the other hand, focus on assessing the quality of healthcare services and facilities from the patient’s perspective, identifying areas for improvement in the patient experience.

When used together, PROMs and PREMs offer healthcare providers valuable insights for enhancing patient care, pinpointing service improvements, and measuring treatment effectiveness. This integrated approach is crucial for delivering optimal patient care and continuously improving healthcare services.
