VBHC evolution: navigating challenges and seizing future opportunities


This topic delves into the evolving landscape of VBHC, presenting pivotal challenges to implementation and solutions to these, as well as promising future directions of VBHC.

VBHC Implementation Challenges

Advancements in technology, policy, and healthcare delivery models will significantly influence the evolution of VBHC, making care more personalised, efficient, and patient centred. The objective is to enable a deeper understanding of patient needs and outcomes, leading to a new set of VBHC KPIs, complementing existing measures and creating more effective and holistic care strategies.

Challenge:

Many healthcare providers and organisations resist changing traditional fee for service models to VBHC due to ingrained practices, scepticism and fear of change.

Solution:

Education (including hands on training) and engagement initiatives that demonstrate VBHC’s benefits for patient outcomes and system efficiency can help shift mindsets.

Challenge:

Healthcare providers worry about potential negative impact on income when moving from volume based to value based models, fearing reduced earnings.

Challenge:

Demonstrate through pilot programs and case studies how VBHC reimbursement models, including shared savings, can foster sustainable healthcare practices and equitable compensation, rewarding providers for efficiency improvements and better patient outcomes.

Challenge:

Establishing and agreeing on standardised, meaningful patient reported outcome measures that reflect true value to patients is difficult.

Solution:

Collaborating with healthcare pioneers and patient groups to define essential patient reported health outcomes across biological, social, mental and physiological dimensions, ensuring these metrics are universally applied.

Challenge:

Misaligned incentives between payers, providers, and patients can hinder the implementation of VBHC models.

Challenge:

Developing new payment models, such as bundled payments or capitation, that align stakeholders’ interests towards improved patient outcomes.

Challenge:

Integrating disparate health information systems, including legacy systems, to effectively measure and utilise data for VBHC is a significant challenge.

Solution:

Investing in interoperable IT infrastructure (including VBHC integratable platforms) and adopting universal data standards facilitate seamless data sharing and analysis.

Challenge:

The regular collection of patient data poses perceived burdens for providers, patients, and administrators due to the need for consistent data collection, storage, security and management across systems.

Challenge:

Streamlining and automating data collection processes with integrated IT solutions and patient portals can reduce the perceived burden, making data collection more efficient and less intrusive, while ensuring data security and compliance.

Challenge:

The success of VBHC implementation can be significantly influenced by government policies and regulations, which may either facilitate or obstruct the transition.

Solution:

Advocating for policies that support VBHC principles and working closely with regulatory bodies can create a conducive environment for VBHC. Implementing a top down approach ensures alignment of healthcare goals across all levels, from policy to patient care.

Predictive analytics​

AI/ML technologies allow the prediction of health outcomes, enabling care tailored to individual patient needs.

Data driven decisions

Smart decision support tools will leverage big data analysis for improved decision making and outcome management in VBHC.

Chronic condition management

Digital wearables and IoTs facilitate real time monitoring and management of chronic diseases, improving accessibility and patient quality of life.

Diagnostic accuracy

Smart algorithms, reinforced with big data, enable more accurate and efficient diagnosis, reducing unnecessary procedures and focusing on value.

Personalised treatment plans

The capability to cross correlate medical, mental, environmental, historical and outcomes data will facilitate personalised holistic care strategies.

Patient engagement tools

Smart platforms and patient portals will boost patient involvement and education for better health outcomes and adherence.
