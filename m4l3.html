The VBHC journey:
A roadmap to care delivery transformation


This topic explores the hands on integration of VBHC across healthcare levels. We outline strategic steps for applying VBHC from individual Medical Practices to national systems, focusing on effective methodologies for real world implementation – practically transforming VBHC from an idea to an actionable reality.

Innovative Care Models In VBHC

VBHC pioneers are shifting towards care models that ensure high quality, patient centred, and cost effective services.

Integrated Practice Units (IPUs)

Teams that provide full cycle care for a patient’s condition, enhancing coordination and outcomes.

Patient Centred Medical Homes (PCMHs)

Emphasising primary care as the central component for managing patient health, focusing on accessibility, long term relationships and comprehensive care.

Accountable Care Organisations (ACOs)

Groups of doctors, hospitals, payers and other health care providers who voluntarily come together to provide coordinated high quality care to their patients.

Telehealth and digital health services

Utilising technology to deliver care and monitor patient health remotely, increasing equity, accessibility and efficiency.

Blueprint Strategies For Integrating VBHC In Healthcare Settings

Navigate the path to seamless VBHC integration with our strategic blueprint, exploring the intricacies of adopting and optimising VBHC principles effectively.

Strategy 1

Cultivating leadership and culture

Empower leaders to champion VBHC, fostering a culture that values patient reported outcomes over volume, through education and shared vision.

Strategy 2

Redesigning care models

Revamp care processes and pathways to prioritise patient reported outcomes, using innovative models like integrated care teams and patient centred medical homes, for more value focused services.

Strategy 3

Aligning financial models

Adjust financial incentives to support VBHC goals, encouraging practices that prioritise quality care and patient outcomes.

Strategy 4

Integrating technology for impact

Utilise digital health technologies for efficient data collection and analysis, enhancing outcome measurement and patient care – all while reducing provider burdens.

Strategy 5

Engaging patients and communities

Combine patient and community feedback with healthcare delivery for better outcomes, experiences and satisfaction. This involves shared decision making using PROMs to ensure care meets patient needs and values.

Strategy 6

Fostering interdisciplinary collaboration

Promote inter healthcare disciplines teamwork for coordinated, patient centred care, in line with VBHC principles.

Strategy 7

Leveraging data to measure success

Implement systems for robust outcome measurement through new KPIs and metrics essential for understanding the impact of VBHC implementations. This demonstrates how data informs care delivery and continuous value improvement.

Strategy 8

Ensuring care coordination

Streamline care coordination efforts, particularly for complex needs, ensuring comprehensive and efficient patient care.

Innovative Reimbursement Models In VBHC

Transitioning to VBHC necessitates innovative reimbursement models that prioritise patient outcomes and system efficiency.

Bundled payments​

A single price for all services during an episode of care, incentivising cost control and quality improvement.

Capitation

Providers are paid a set fee per patient per period, promoting preventive care and managing chronic conditions effectively.

Pay for performance

Compensation is directly tied to achieving specific health outcomes, enhancing accountability for patient health.

Shared savings programs

Providers share in savings they generate for the payer (e.g., insurance companies) by meeting predefined cost and quality benchmarks.
