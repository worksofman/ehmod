The amalgamation of wearable tech, mobile health apps, and digital therapeutics has led to a more engaged and informed patient population. These technologies provide individuals with real time insights, continuous monitoring, and evidence based interventions. They facilitate a patient centric approach, and a seamless interaction between individuals and healthcare providers, promoting proactive management of health, adherence to treatment plans, and a data driven approach to healthcare.

Big Data and AI in Digital Health signify the collection, analysis, and application of large volumes of healthcare data using intelligent algorithms to derive actionable insights. This amalgamation enables predictive analytics, enhanced decision making, and personalised care. By analysing patterns and trends in data, AI can support early diagnosis, optimise treatment plans, and improve operational efficiencies, paving the way for a more proactive and data driven healthcare ecosystem.

Meet Dr. Eric Schadt from the Icahn Institute, who is pioneering the utilisation of big data to craft the medical systems of the future. Discover how AI algorithms are being developed to detect ailments and construct personalised health profiles.

3D printing is a process that creates three dimensional objects from a digital model by layering material in successive layers. In the healthcare sector, this technology has revolutionised the way medical devices, prosthetics, and even tissue engineering are approached. It enables the creation of customised medical products like dental implants, orthopaedic devices, prosthetic limbs, and medical models used for surgical preparation.

Delve into the realm of bioprinting with Taneka Jones, a groundbreaking technique of 3D printing utilising bioink infused with living cells. Amid the dire shortage of donor organs, with countless individuals on transplant waitlists for life saving organs like hearts, kidneys, and livers, bioprinting emerges as a beacon of hope.
